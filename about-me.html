About Me

Sin Prelić

I'm a PhD-graduate versatile biologist with extensive international lab experience, cross-disciplinary expertise and a portfolio of publications in scientific journals and talks at international specialist conferences.

Trained chiefly in molecular cell biology and neuroscience, I now have over a decade of experience in various life science research labs, including university, institute and hospital environments. I'm comfortable navigating between biological disciplines, and personally strive for inventive and original output. My work always reflects this ethic and cross-disciplinarity. So far, it has spanned areas like molecular biology and bioinformatics, cytology, ecology, sensory neuroscience, epigenetics, molecular technique development, animal behavior, microbe physiology and evolution.

In addition to having spent many years running experiments in labs, I've led and managed research projects, frequently worked with large -omics and multidimensional datasets, involved myself in community organization, taught university courses and supervised students, designed and tested novel biological probes, written many scripts for data analysis, all the while writing and illustrating and enjoying getting technical ideas across elegantly.

In short, I relish in learning, trying things for their own sake, and applying my breadth of experience.

Grilled anything. The practice of grilling requires the confluence of eyes-for-detail, a grasp of thermodynamics, and a steady calculating forbearance.
